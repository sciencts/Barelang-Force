Kontes Robot Terbang Indonesia (KRTI)

KRTI (Indonesian Flying Robot Contest) is a competition in Indonesia organized by the Ministry of Education, Culture, Research, and Technology (Kemendikbudristek). This contest aims to hone students' creativity in designing, making, and controlling flying robots, such as unmanned aircraft (drones). KRTI also functions as a competition event that fosters innovation in the field of aviation technology, while supporting the development of the Indonesian aerospace industry.

The objectives of the Indonesian Flying Robot Contest (KRTI) are as follows:

  1. Encourage Aviation Technology Innovation: KRTI aims to stimulate the development of new and efficient aviation technologies.
  2. Sharpen Students' Technical Skills: The contest enhances students' skills in areas such as aerodynamics, electronics, and software engineering.
  3. Promote Research and Development (R&D): KRTI supports research that contributes to drone technology advancement in Indonesia.
  4. Increase National Competitiveness in the Global Arena: The contest helps develop talents that boost Indonesia's global competitiveness in aviation technology.
  5. Produce Technological Solutions for Various Problems: Participants are encouraged to create solutions for real-world challenges using drone technology.
  6. Build Networks and Collaboration: KRTI fosters collaboration between students, academics, and industry professionals.

Categories of Indonesian Flying Robot Contest (KRTI):

  1. Fixed Wing: This category involves designing and operating drones with fixed-wing configurations, often used for mapping or reconnaissance missions.
  2. Racing Plane: A speed and agility competition where participants race their drones through a predefined track as quickly as possible.
  3. Technology Development: Focuses on innovative technological developments in aviation and drone technology, including hardware and software solutions.
  4. Vertical Take-Off and Landing (VTOL): Competitors design drones that can take off and land vertically, suitable for missions in difficult terrains.
  5. Long Endurance Low Altitude: Drones are designed for long endurance at low altitude, ideal for continuous monitoring or patrol missions.

Overview of Indonesian Flying Robot Contest (KRTI)

  • The Indonesian Flying Robot Contest (KRTI) was first held in 2013 by the Ministry of Education, Culture, Research, and Technology (Kemendikbudristek). Since then, the contest has been held annually, with the aim of promoting technological innovation and improving students' technical skills in the field of flying robots. If calculated from the beginning of its implementation in 2013 until now, KRTI has been running for 11 years in 2024.

    • Indonesian Flying Robot Contest (KRTI) 2024

  • The Indonesian Flying Robot Contest (KRTI) 2024 will be held at the Vocational Faculty of Yogyakarta State University (UNY) in Gunungkidul. This event takes place from September 12 to 18, 2024, and is attended by 100 teams from 42 leading universities in Indonesia. KRTI aims to promote technological innovation and enhance students' technical skills in the field of flying robots and drones.
